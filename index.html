Tutoring Style

My main focus is helping students develop problem-solving skills for math competitions and olympiad-style contests. I work with motivated students who want to move beyond standard school mathematics and learn how to approach challenging problems more independently.

I usually meet with students once or twice a week for about two hours. Between sessions, it is important that students are able to dedicate at least 8 to 10 hours to independent practice. This outside work is an essential part of preparation. For students who are willing to study more seriously, I am happy to provide additional problems and reading material.

A typical session usually follows this structure:

  1. We begin by reviewing the previously assigned homework, especially the problems the student found difficult or could not complete.
  2. We then introduce the topic of the week, usually through a short discussion, warm-up problems, and some focused lecturing when needed.
  3. Most of the session is spent working through a selected set of problems on that topic. The student does most of the problem solving, while I guide the discussion, ask questions, and help develop useful strategies.
  4. After the session, I assign a follow-up problem set related to the topic of the week, possibly accompanied by some proof-writing practice depending on the student's needs and level.

Depending on the student's background, goals, and needs, we may also follow a book or a longer-term study plan. I usually decide this after meeting the student and understanding their strengths, weaknesses, and contest goals.

I currently offer tutoring online over Zoom, but I am also open to in-person tutoring in the Toronto area. For online tutoring, students need a tablet and pen so that we can write and work through problems simultaneously during the session.
